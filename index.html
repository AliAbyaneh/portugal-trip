🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🦐 Marisqueira de Esmoriz

Hit this on the way — fishermen's marisqueira packed with locals. Fresh grilled sardines, amêijoas à bulhão pato (clams in garlic & coriander), and whatever came off the boats that morning. Shellfish platters ~C$18–25, grilled fish ~C$10–15.

📍 Esmoriz (on route, before Ovar)

🐙 Restaurante A Cozinha do Manel

Order the arroz de marisco (seafood rice with prawns, clams & crab — feeds two easily) or polvo à lagareiro (whole octopus roasted with olive oil & smashed potatoes). Arroz de marisco ~C$22 for two.

📍 Ovar town center

🐟 Restaurante Marisol — Espinho

Mid-ride refuel. Grilled robalo (sea bass) or dourada (gilt-head bream) straight off the Atlantic. Fish is weighed before grilling — point at what looks good. ~C$12–16/kg.

📍 Espinho promenade (on route)

🦐 Cervejaria Praia da Granja

Quick stop for a plate of camarão (prawns) and an ice-cold Super Bock. Also does grilled sardinhas when in season. Prawns ~C$8, beer ~C$2.

📍 Granja beach (on route)

🍰 Confeitaria Pão-de-Ló de Ovar

Dessert: the iconic Ovar sponge cake — intentionally undercooked, gooey & custardy inside. PGI-protected! ~C$4 per cake.

📍 Rua Elias Garcia, Ovar

🐟 Seafood tip: The Espinho/Ovar coast is a working fishing region. Look for "peixe grelhado" signs — restaurants near the harbours serve whatever's freshest. April is great for sardines, robalo, and carapau (horse mackerel).
Route tip: Mostly flat coastal riding. You'll pass through Espinho and along boardwalks. Leave Porto early to enjoy stops along the way. The lagoon detour adds beautiful scenery.

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🦞 Salpoente

Former salt warehouse on the canal. Creative seafood tasting menus — deconstructed caldeirada, sea urchin, Atlantic prawns with Ria de Aveiro salt. Splurge-worthy. Tasting menu ~C$55. Reserve ahead.

📍 Cais de São Roque 83, Aveiro

🐙 O Bairro

Beloved petiscos spot — order the polvo grelhado (grilled octopus), amêijoas à bulhão pato (clams in garlic & coriander), and pataniscas de bacalhau (cod fritters). Pair with vinho verde. Petiscos ~C$5–10 each.

📍 Rua do Tenente Resende, Aveiro

🐟 O Mercado do Peixe

Right by the fish market — freshest catch in town, grilled to order. Try the caldeirada de enguias (eel stew) — an Aveiro regional specialty you won't find elsewhere. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Near Mercado do Peixe, Aveiro

🦐 Casa de Chá — Costa Nova

Beachside with striped houses as your backdrop. Fresh grilled sardines, arroz de marisco, camarão grelhado (grilled prawns) with garlic butter. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Costa Nova do Prado

🍰 Ovos Moles

Dessert: Aveiro's signature sweet — egg yolk & sugar in wafer shells shaped like fish & shells. PGI-protected! From Confeitaria Peixinho ~C$8/box.

📍 Multiple shops, Rua João Mendonça

🐟 Aveiro seafood tip: The Ria de Aveiro lagoon produces unique seafood — eel (enguias), clams, and sea bass farmed in salt pans. Ask for "enguias fritas" (fried eel) or "caldeirada de enguias" — an Aveiro classic you won't find south of here.
Important: Ferry crossing at Ria de Aveiro is essential for the route. Check times in advance at moveaveiro.pt — +351 234 380 560

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🦐 Marisqueira O Navegante

Figueira's best marisqueira near the port. The cataplana de marisco (copper-pot seafood stew with clams, prawns & fish) is magnificent. Also: arroz de tamboril (monkfish rice, creamy & rich). Cataplana for two ~C$28.

📍 Near Porto de Pesca, Figueira da Foz

🐟 Restaurante Caçarola 1

Locals-only feel. Famous for grilled dourada and robalo — fish is weighed, charcoal-grilled whole, served with boiled potatoes & olive oil. The way it should be. Cash preferred. Mains ~C$10–15.

📍 Rua Dr. Francisco Diniz, Figueira da Foz

🐙 O Escondidinho

Hidden gem near the port. Polvo à lagareiro (roast octopus), caldeirada do Mondego (mixed river & sea fish stew — unique to this area), and fresh grilled carapau. Mains ~C$10–15.

📍 Near Porto de Pesca, Figueira da Foz

🐟 Tasca do Celso — Praia de Mira

Perfect mid-ride lunch. Grilled sardines straight from the Arte Xávega boats on the beach. Paper tablecloths, cold beer, zero pretension. Lunch ~C$10–12.

📍 Praia de Mira (on route)

🏪 Mercado Municipal de Figueira

Morning fish market — see the freshest Atlantic catch being sold. Grab pastéis de bacalhau from a nearby café, or have the market grill your purchase at a neighbouring tasca.

📍 Mercado Municipal, Figueira da Foz

🐟 Figueira seafood tip: Figueira sits where the Mondego River meets the Atlantic — try caldeirada do Mondego, a unique stew mixing river fish AND ocean fish. The port area has the freshest and cheapest restaurants.
Route tip: A scenic day through coastal lagoons and pine forests. The route passes through Praia de Mira — good lunch stop. The final stretch into Figueira da Foz has some elevation near Cape Mondego.

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🐟 Restaurante A Celeste

THE caldeirada nazarena spot — Nazaré's legendary fish stew with layers of fish, potatoes, tomatoes & saffron. This is the dish that defines Nazaré. Also killer grilled sardines. Caldeirada ~C$16, grilled fish ~C$12.

📍 Avenida da República, Nazaré

🦞 Sitiado

Take the funicular up to Sítio. Arroz de lavagante (lobster rice) with Atlantic views — creamy rice packed with lobster meat, served in the pot. Try percebes (goose barnacles) if in season. Lobster rice for two ~C$35.

📍 Sítio de Nazaré (top of funicular)

🦐 Maria do Mar

Beachfront, family-run. The açorda de marisco (bread-thickened seafood stew with prawns, clams & coriander) is a Nazaré classic. Also: cataplana de peixe e marisco in the copper pot. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Praia da Nazaré

🐙 Taberna d'Adélia

Tiny taberna, no frills. Petiscos heaven: polvo à feira (octopus with olive oil & paprika), camarão ao alho (garlic prawns), pataniscas de bacalhau (cod fritters). Local white wine. Petiscos ~C$4–8 each.

📍 Nazaré old town

🐟 O Casalinho — S. Pedro de Moel

Mid-ride lunch. Grilled robalo (sea bass) caught that morning, served whole with boiled potatoes & olive oil. Simple, perfect. Also has excellent caldeirada. Lunch ~C$10–12.

📍 S. Pedro de Moel (on route)

🐟 Nazaré = Seafood Heaven: Portugal's most famous fishing town. Dried fish hanging on racks everywhere. Caldeirada nazarena is THE dish — order it and compare. Side streets behind the beach have the real spots where fishermen eat.
Big day alert: 60 km through pine forests and coastal villages. Start early, pack snacks, and take breaks at the beach villages. The terrain is mostly flat.

Óbidos

Magical medieval walled town — walk the walls, explore the castle, drink Ginjinha (cherry liquor) from a chocolate cup!

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🦀 Nau dos Corvos

Dramatically perched on Peniche's cliffs at Cabo Carvoeiro. Fresh seafood with crashing waves below. The percebes (goose barnacles) here are incredible — harvested from these very rocks. Also: arroz de marisco. Percebes ~C$25, mains ~C$15–20.

📍 Cabo Carvoeiro, Peniche

🐟 Tasca do Joel

Authentic fishermen's tasca near the port. Caldeirada à moda de Peniche (THE local fish stew) is the star — ask for it. Cash only, paper tablecloths, the real deal. Mains ~C$10–14.

📍 Near Peniche harbour

🦐 Restaurante Sardinha

Family-run, specializing in grilled sardines and arroz de marisco. The sardinhas assadas (charcoal-grilled sardines) with roasted peppers is peak Portugal. Mains ~C$10–15.

📍 Peniche center

🐙 Marisqueira Mirandum

Portside marisqueira. Go for the cataplana de tamboril e gambas (monkfish & prawn copper-pot stew) or the whole grilled robalo. Views of the fishing boats. Cataplana for two ~C$30.

📍 Porto de Pesca, Peniche

🦐 Peniche seafood tip: Peniche is a major fishing port — some of the freshest and cheapest seafood on the entire coast. Caldeirada à moda de Peniche is THE local dish. Every family has their recipe. The port-side restaurants serve what came in that morning.
Pro tip: Óbidos is a must-stop. The medieval town is ~5 km off route but absolutely worth the detour. Try Ginjinha served in an edible chocolate cup!

Day 6 — Rest Day in Peniche

0 kmApril 8, 2026Recovery & Exploration
Things to Do

Berlengas Island Day Trip

UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Take the boat from Peniche harbour (~30 min). Visit the Fortress of S. João Baptista, snorkel in crystal waters, hike the island trails. Book ahead!

Peniche Fortress

16th-century fortress — was a political prison during the dictatorship. Now a museum with powerful exhibits.

Bilros Lace

Peniche is famous for traditional bobbin lace (Renda de Bilros). Watch artisans at work or buy handmade pieces.

Watch Surfing at Supertubos

Walk or short ride to Supertubos — one of Europe's best beach breaks

Seafood Day

Peniche is a major fishing port. Treat yourself to fresh fish, caldeirada (fish stew), or percebes (barnacles).

Bike Shops (maintenance day)

Ferbike

Peniche — good time for a mid-trip tune-up

Sbikeshop

Peniche — parts and repairs

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood Feast Day 🐟

🦞 Cervejaria Vasco da Gama

Local cervejaria with excellent shellfish platters. Amêijoas (clams), sapateira recheada (stuffed crab), camarão tigre grelhado (grilled tiger prawns). Casual, noisy, authentic. Platters ~C$20–30.

📍 Peniche center

🐟 Restaurante Mirandum

Rest day = splurge day. Full arroz de lavagante (lobster rice) overlooking the harbour. Or go for the caldeirada real — caldeirada with extra shellfish. Lobster rice for two ~C$40.

📍 Porto de Pesca, Peniche

🦀 Percebes at the Port

Walk along the port and find a tasca selling percebes (goose barnacles) by the plate — these bizarre, prehistoric-looking crustaceans are one of Portugal's most prized delicacies. Peel, dip in the briny juice, eat. ~C$20–30/plate.

📍 Porto de Pesca, Peniche

🏪 Mercado Municipal de Peniche

Morning fish market — see the lota (fish auction). Grab pastéis de bacalhau (cod croquettes) from a nearby café. Watch local fishermen's wives selling the catch. ~C$1.50 each.

📍 Mercado Municipal, Peniche

🐟 Rest day seafood strategy: This is your chance to do a full seafood crawl. Morning: fish market. Lunch: caldeirada at a port-side tasca. Afternoon: percebes and beer. Dinner: arroz de lavagante or cataplana. You're in one of Portugal's best fishing ports — make it count!
Mid-trip check: Great day to inspect your bike — check tire pressure, brake pads, chain wear, and tighten bolts. Still ~110 km to Lisbon!

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🐟 Prim

Local favourite in Ericeira's old town. The arroz de polvo (octopus rice) and fresh grilled fish are outstanding. This fishing village takes its seafood seriously. Mains ~C$14–20.

📍 Rua Capitão João Lopes, Ericeira

🦐 Mar à Vista

Seafood restaurant with actual sea views. Known for arroz de marisco and grilled dourada (gilt-head bream). Try the cataplana de peixe — Ericeira's surf culture meets traditional fishing village. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Rua Dr. Eduardo Burnay, Ericeira

🐟 Tik Tak

Hole-in-the-wall tasca beloved by surfers and locals. Massive portions of grilled fish at tiny prices. The prato do dia often features fresh catch — sardines, carapau, or robalo. ~C$8–10 with soup, bread & drink.

📍 Ericeira old town

🐙 Restaurante Peixe à Moda de Ericeira

The house specialty — ericeira-style fish baked with potatoes, onions, peppers & olive oil in a clay pot. A regional dish unique to this village. Also: caldeirada and fresh grilled percebes. Mains ~C$12–16.

📍 Ericeira

🐟 Marisqueira Furnas — Santa Cruz

If you stop in Santa Cruz en route — cliffside marisqueira with incredible fresh shellfish. The gambas à guilho (garlic prawns) are addictive. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Santa Cruz (on route)

🐟 Ericeira seafood tip: Ericeira is a World Surfing Reserve but it was a fishing village first. The old harbour area has the most authentic spots. Look for "peixe do dia" (fish of the day) — it means it came off the boats that morning.

Transport Notes

Cascais commuter line — bikes allowed outside rush hour. Useful to skip a busy section into Lisbon.

Transtejo Ferry

If approaching from south bank, take the ferry across the Tagus to Lisbon. Scenic and practical!

🍽️ Where to Eat — Seafood & Local Picks 🐟

🦞 Cervejaria Ramiro

THE seafood institution — locals and chefs agree it's Lisbon's best shellfish. Tiger prawns, percebes (goose barnacles), amêijoas, camarão, and finish with a prego (steak sandwich). Worth every cent. Budget ~C$40–55/person.

📍 Av. Almirante Reis 1H, Intendente

🐙 O Velho Eurico — Alfama

Quintessential Alfama tasca. Paper tablecloths, wine from the barrel, grilled sardines and polvo à lagareiro. Sometimes fado plays. This is the real Lisbon. Grilled fish ~C$10–14.

📍 Largo São Cristóvão 3, Alfama

🐟 Ponto Final — Cacilhas

Take the ferry across the Tagus to Cacilhas for this legendary waterfront spot. Grilled fish, arroz de marisco, stunning views back at Lisbon. Arrive early — no reservations, always a queue. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Rua do Ginjal 72, Cacilhas (ferry from Cais do Sodré)

🦐 Marisqueira Última Ceia — Cascais

If passing through Cascais — locals' favourite for shellfish. Cataplana de marisco (copper-pot stew), grilled robalo, and superb percebes. Away from the tourist waterfront. Mains ~C$15–25.

📍 Cascais backstreets

🍰 Pastéis de Belém

THE original pastel de nata since 1837. The recipe is a closely guarded secret. Get them warm with cinnamon & powdered sugar. ~C$2 each. Eat inside to skip the line.

📍 Rua de Belém 84–92, Belém

🐟 Taberna da Rua das Flores

Tiny, no-reservations petiscos bar in Chiado. Incredible seafood petiscos — polvo, gambas, seasonal fish. Arrive by 7pm or wait 45min+. Petiscos ~C$5–12.

📍 Rua das Flores 103, Chiado

🐟 Lisbon seafood strategy: Cervejaria Ramiro for shellfish blowout. Ponto Final for grilled fish with a view. Taberna da Rua das Flores for petiscos. Avoid Rua Augusta — best food is always one street back. Look for "menu do dia" signs for ~C$10–12 full meals.
