🗺️ Trip Overview

8 riding days + 1 rest day cycling the Portuguese Atlantic coast from Porto to Lisbon along EuroVelo 1. Rest day in Peniche. Mix of coastal paths, lagoons, pine forests, and historic towns. Arriving Lisbon April 10.

Day 1 — Porto → Ovar

📏 60 km📅 April 3, 2026📍 EuroVelo 1 · Section 26 (with lagoon detour)
🔗 EuroVelo Section 26 Details
🏨 Accommodation

Ovar Youth Hostel

Avenida D. Manuel I, Ovar

📞 +351 256 591 832

Part of Pousadas de Juventude network. Budget-friendly, bike storage available.

pousadasjuventude.pt
🎯 Highlights & Things to Do

⛪ Porto Start

Clérigos Tower, Lello Bookstore, Ribeira district, Stock Exchange Palace, São Bento Station (azulejo tiles)

🍷 Vila Nova de Gaia

Port Wine Cellars, Serra do Pilar Monastery — cross the Luís I Bridge for stunning views

⛪ Senhor da Pedra Chapel

Hexagonal chapel on rocks at Miramar Beach — iconic photo stop

🐦 Paramos Lagoon

Bird Observatory — flamingos and wading birds in the coastal lagoon

🏖️ Furadouro Beach

Sandy beach near Ovar, great for an end-of-day stroll

🍰 Pão-de-Ló de Ovar

Famous fluffy sponge cake — the local specialty. Don't leave without trying it!

🔧 Bike Shops

Garagem Paciência

📞 +351 256 040 455

Ovar — repairs and basic parts

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Confeitaria Pão-de-Ló de Ovar

The iconic Ovar specialty — moist, gooey sponge cake unlike any other. This bakery has been making the traditional recipe for generations. ~C$4 per cake.

📍 Rua Elias Garcia, Ovar

Restaurante A Cozinha do Manel

Local tasca with hearty portions. Try the arroz de marisco (seafood rice) or polvo à lagareiro (octopus with olive oil & roast potatoes). Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Ovar town center

Marisqueira de Esmoriz

Just before Ovar — a fishermen's favourite for fresh grilled fish and shellfish. Packed with locals at lunch. Grilled fish ~C$10–15.

📍 Esmoriz (on route)

Cervejaria Praia da Granja

If you stop in Espinho or Granja along the way — fresh bifanas (pork sandwiches) and cold Super Bock. The cyclist's perfect mid-ride fuel. ~C$5.

📍 Espinho / Granja (on route)

🍰 Don't miss: Pão-de-Ló de Ovar is unlike regular sponge cake — it's intentionally undercooked in the center, creating a gooey, custardy interior. It has PGI (Protected Geographical Indication) status!
💡 Route tip: Mostly flat coastal riding. You'll pass through Espinho and along boardwalks. Leave Porto early to enjoy stops along the way. The lagoon detour adds beautiful scenery.

Day 2 — Ovar → Costa Nova

📏 40 km📅 April 4, 2026📍 EuroVelo 1 · Section 25 + part of 24
🔗 Sec 25 · Sec 24
🏨 Accommodation

Aveiro Youth Hostel

Rua das Pombas, Aveiro

📞 +351 234 482 233

Central location, walking distance to canals and town center. Stay here and ride to Costa Nova in the morning, or stay in Costa Nova itself.

🎯 Highlights & Things to Do

🌿 São Jacinto Dunes Reserve

Natural reserve with boardwalk trails through coastal dunes and pine forest

🚴 Torreira

Highest bike usage per capita in all of Portugal! You'll fit right in.

🌊 Ria de Aveiro

Stunning lagoon system — salt pans, fishing boats, and birdlife

⛴️ Ferry Crossing

Forte da Barra ↔ São Jacinto ferry. Check schedule at moveaveiro.pt or call +351 234 380 560

🏠 Costa Nova Striped Houses

Iconic candy-striped Palheiros (wooden houses) — the most Instagrammed spot on the coast!

🚣 Aveiro Canals & Moliceiros

Take a ride on a moliceiro boat through Aveiro's picturesque canals — the "Venice of Portugal"

🏛️ Ílhavo Maritime Museum

Excellent museum about cod fishing history and maritime heritage — near Costa Nova

🔧 Bike Shops

Aveiro Bike Shops

Pernalonga · Bike Zone · Biclaria · Guersan · Pabol · Zé das Bikes — multiple options in town

Murtosa Area

CicloPedal 📞 +351 916 457 602 · Giro Café (bike-friendly stop)

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Salpoente

Set in a former salt warehouse — creative Portuguese tasting menus using local ingredients. Splurge-worthy but unforgettable. Tasting menu ~C$55. Reservations essential.

📍 Cais de São Roque 83, Aveiro

O Bairro

Beloved local spot for petiscos (Portuguese tapas). Try the rojões (braised pork) and alheira frita (fried sausage). Casual, affordable, packed with locals. Petiscos ~C$5–10 each.

📍 Rua do Tenente Resende, Aveiro

Tasca do Confrade

Traditional tasca with excellent bacalhau (cod) dishes and local wines by the glass. Try bacalhau com natas (cod with cream). Mains ~C$12–16.

📍 Aveiro old town

Casa de Chá — Costa Nova

Beachside spot in Costa Nova for fresh grilled sardines and caldeirada (fish stew). Eat with a view of the striped houses. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Costa Nova do Prado

Ovos Moles — anywhere!

Aveiro's signature sweet — egg yolk and sugar paste in delicate wafer shells shaped like fish, shells & barrels. Buy from Confeitaria Peixinho (~C$8/box). They have PGI status!

📍 Multiple shops along Rua João Mendonça

⛴️ Important: Ferry crossing at Ria de Aveiro is essential for the route. Check times in advance at moveaveiro.pt — +351 234 380 560

Day 3 — Costa Nova → Figueira da Foz

📏 50 km📅 April 5, 2026📍 EuroVelo 1 · Sections 24 + 23
🔗 Sec 24 · Sec 23
🏨 Accommodation

Figueira da Foz — Tourist Info

Avenida 25 de Abril, Figueira da Foz

📞 +351 233 422 610

Contact tourist office for hostel/pension recommendations in your budget. Multiple options €30–50.

🎯 Highlights — Through Praia de Mira

🏖️ Barra Lighthouse

Tallest lighthouse in Portugal (62m). Visible from far along the coast — great morning photo stop.

🎣 Vagueira Beach

Watch Arte Xávega — traditional ox-drawn fishing technique still practiced here

🏖️ Praia de Mira

Charming beach village with colorful fishing boats. Praia de Mira Youth Hostel (📞 +351 231 480 040) available if you want to split this day.

🌿 Vela Lagoon

Peaceful lagoon with elevated wooden walkways through wetlands

🎯 Highlights — Approaching Figueira da Foz

🏖️ Cape Mondego Lighthouse

Scenic lighthouse perched on the cape with panoramic ocean views

🏴‍☠️ Bandeira Viewpoint

250m elevation — historical pirate lookout point! Incredible coastal panorama.

🏖️ Quiaios Beach

Wild, unspoiled beach backed by dunes and pine forest

🌲 Leiria Pine Forest

Ancient royal forest — trees originally planted to supply wood for Portuguese caravels

🔧 Bike Shops

Casa Biscaia

📞 +351 233 423 048

Figueira da Foz — established bike shop for repairs and parts

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Restaurante Caçarola 1

Locals-only feel, generous portions. Famous for arroz de tamboril (monkfish rice) and grilled fish of the day. Cash preferred. Mains ~C$10–15.

📍 Rua Dr. Francisco Diniz, Figueira da Foz

Marisqueira Ramiro (Figueira)

No relation to the Lisbon one — but equally loved locally for fresh seafood. Try the amêijoas à bulhão pato (clams in garlic & coriander). Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Near the fish market, Figueira da Foz

Tasca do Celso — Praia de Mira

Perfect lunch stop mid-ride. Simple grilled fish, cold beer, paper tablecloths. The real deal. Lunch ~C$10–12.

📍 Praia de Mira (on route)

Restaurante Teimoso

Old-school Figueira restaurant. Try the chanfana (slow-cooked goat in red wine) — a Beira Litoral classic. Hearty and perfect after a long ride. Mains ~C$10–14.

📍 Figueira da Foz center

🐟 Local tip: Figueira da Foz has an excellent fish market (Mercado Municipal). Arrive early and some stalls sell ready-to-eat petiscos. The town is known for arroz de marisco and caldeirada.
💡 Route tip: A scenic day through coastal lagoons and pine forests. The route passes through Praia de Mira — good lunch stop. The final stretch into Figueira da Foz has some elevation near Cape Mondego.

📏 60 km📅 April 6, 2026📍 Sections 22 + 21 + 20💪 Big day
🔗 Sec 22 · Sec 21 · Sec 20
🏨 Accommodation

Nazaré Accommodation

Multiple options near the Nazaré Bullring area. Guesthouses and hostels €25–50/night.

Check Booking.com or Hostelworld for best rates in April.

🎯 Highlights — Section 22 (Figueira da Foz area)

🌊 Mondego River Estuary

Where the river meets the sea — beautiful marshland scenery

🏝️ Morraceira Island

Salt marshes and rice paddies — unique landscape for coastal Portugal

⛪ Seiça Monastery

Atmospheric ruins of a Cistercian monastery

🏖️ Claridade Beach & River Beach of Ereira

Two different beach vibes — ocean and river

🎯 Highlights — Section 21 (Pine Forest)

🏖️ Vieira de Leiria Beach

Laid-back fishing village beach

🌲 Bear National Forest

Pine trees planted centuries ago to build Portuguese caravels for the Age of Discovery!

💧 Ervedeira Lagoon

Peaceful freshwater lagoon, great for a break

🎯 Highlights — Section 20 (approaching Nazaré)

🏖️ S. Pedro de Moel

Charming village with lighthouse, beach, and pine forest setting

🏛️ Marinha Grande Glass Museum

Celebrating the region's centuries-old glass-making tradition

🌊 Dramatic Cliffs & Dunes

The coastline becomes increasingly dramatic as you approach Nazaré

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Restaurante A Celeste

One of Nazaré's best-kept secrets among locals. Upstairs from the beach, incredible caldeirada nazarena (Nazaré-style fish stew) and grilled sardines. Mains ~C$12–16.

📍 Avenida da República, Nazaré

Taberna d'Adélia

Tiny, no-frills taberna in the old town. Petiscos and local wine. Try the pataniscas de bacalhau (cod fritters) and peixinhos da horta (fried green beans — the original tempura!). Petiscos ~C$4–8.

📍 Rua das Flores, Nazaré old town

Sitiado

Up in the Sítio neighborhood (take the funicular). Modern take on traditional dishes with stunning ocean views. Try the arroz de lavagante (lobster rice). Mains ~C$15–25.

📍 Sítio de Nazaré

Maria do Mar

Beachfront, family-run. Locals recommend the açorda de marisco (bread-based seafood stew) — a dish you won't find easily outside this coast. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Praia da Nazaré

O Casalinho — S. Pedro de Moel

If you stop in this charming village en route — locals rave about the grilled robalo (sea bass) and the homemade desserts. Lunch special ~C$10.

📍 S. Pedro de Moel (on route)

🐟 Nazaré food tip: Nazaré's fishing heritage means incredible seafood. Look for restaurants where fishermen eat — usually down side streets, not on the main beachfront promenade. Dried fish hanging outside = good sign!
💪 Big day alert: 60 km through pine forests and coastal villages. Start early, pack snacks, and take breaks at the beach villages. The terrain is mostly flat.

Day 5 — Nazaré → Peniche via Óbidos

📏 60 km📅 April 7, 2026📍 Sections 19 + 18
🔗 Sec 19 · Sec 18
🏨 Accommodation

Peniche — Multiple Options

Budget hostels and guesthouses €25–50/night. Surf hostels are plentiful. You'll be here 2 nights (rest day tomorrow!) so choose well.

🎯 Highlights — Section 19 (Nazaré to Foz do Arelho)

🌊 Nazaré Beach & Sítio

Take the inclined funicular lift to the Sítio viewpoint before departing — jaw-dropping views

🏰 Fort of S. Miguel Arcanjo

Iconic lookout over Praia do Norte — where the world record 26.21m wave was surfed!

🏖️ São Martinho do Porto

Stunning shell-shaped bay — like a natural swimming pool

⛰️ Salir do Porto

Portugal's tallest sand dune at 50m! Worth the climb for the views.

🌊 Óbidos Lagoon & Atlantic Viewpoint

Wide lagoon with scenic viewpoints along the ride

🎯 Highlights — Section 18 (Peniche to Foz do Arelho)

🏰 Óbidos

Magical medieval walled town — walk the walls, explore the castle, drink Ginjinha (cherry liquor) from a chocolate cup!

🎨 Caldas da Rainha

Ceramics capital — quirky pottery shops, beautiful D. Carlos I Park

🏄 Supertubos Beach

World Surf League competition venue — perfect barrels

🏰 Fortress of S. João Baptista

On Berlengas Island — accessible by boat from Peniche (save for rest day!)

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Nau dos Corvos

Dramatically perched on the cliffs of Peniche. Fresh seafood with ocean views. Try the percebes (goose barnacles) — expensive but a must-try delicacy. Percebes ~C$25, mains ~C$15–20.

📍 Cabo Carvoeiro, Peniche

Tasca do Joel

Authentic fishermen's tasca near the port. Caldeirada de peixe (fish stew) is the star — this is what locals on Reddit recommend for Peniche. Cash only. Mains ~C$10–14.

📍 Near Peniche harbour

Restaurante Sardinha

Family-run, specializing in — you guessed it — sardines. Also excellent arroz de marisco. Unpretentious, generous portions. Mains ~C$10–15.

📍 Peniche center

1 de Dezembro — Óbidos

Inside the medieval walls. Try the ginjinha in a chocolate cup (C$2) at any stand, then sit down here for a proper meal. Javali (wild boar) stew is the local specialty. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Rua Direita, Óbidos

🦐 Peniche food tip: As a major fishing port, Peniche has some of the freshest and cheapest seafood on the coast. Caldeirada de peixe (fish stew) is THE local dish — every family has their recipe. Ask for "caldeirada à moda de Peniche."
💡 Pro tip: Óbidos is a must-stop. The medieval town is ~5 km off route but absolutely worth the detour. Try Ginjinha served in an edible chocolate cup!

Day 6 — Rest Day in Peniche 🛌

📏 0 km📅 April 8, 2026😴 Recovery & Exploration
🎯 Things to Do

🏝️ Berlengas Island Day Trip

UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Take the boat from Peniche harbour (~30 min). Visit the Fortress of S. João Baptista, snorkel in crystal waters, hike the island trails. Book ahead!

🏰 Peniche Fortress

16th-century fortress — was a political prison during the dictatorship. Now a museum with powerful exhibits.

🧶 Bilros Lace

Peniche is famous for traditional bobbin lace (Renda de Bilros). Watch artisans at work or buy handmade pieces.

🏄 Watch Surfing at Supertubos

Walk or short ride to Supertubos — one of Europe's best beach breaks

🍽️ Seafood Day

Peniche is a major fishing port. Treat yourself to fresh fish, caldeirada (fish stew), or percebes (barnacles).

🔧 Bike Shops (maintenance day!)

Ferbike

Peniche — good time for a mid-trip tune-up

Sbikeshop

Peniche — parts and repairs

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Restaurante Mirandum

Popular with locals for its generous caldeirada and arroz de tamboril. Great views of the harbour. Rest day = splurge day. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Porto de Pesca, Peniche

Cervejaria Vasco da Gama

Local cervejaria (beer hall) with excellent seafood platters. Try the amêijoas (clams) and sapateira recheada (stuffed crab). Casual, noisy, authentic. Mains ~C$10–16.

📍 Peniche center

Mercado Municipal de Peniche

Visit the morning fish market for the freshest catch. Some vendors sell ready-made petiscos. Grab pastéis de bacalhau (cod croquettes) from a nearby café. ~C$1.50 each.

📍 Mercado Municipal, Peniche

🔧 Mid-trip check: Great day to inspect your bike — check tire pressure, brake pads, chain wear, and tighten bolts. Still ~110 km to Lisbon!

🎯 Highlights — Section 17

🦕 Dino Parque — Lourinhã

Largest open-air dinosaur museum in Portugal! Life-size models in a forest setting. Amazing detour (~30 min off route).

🏖️ Santa Cruz Beach

Dramatic cliffs and rock formations. Great beach town for a break.

⛪ Convent of Penafirme

Atmospheric ruins of a medieval convent near the coast

🥾 Maceira Escarpment Walkways

Elevated walkways through dramatic cliff landscapes

⚔️ Battle of Vimeiro

Interpretation Center — where Wellington defeated Napoleon's forces in 1808

🦇 Prehistoric Caves

Cave systems in the region with evidence of prehistoric habitation

🎯 Highlights — Ericeira

🏄 Ericeira

World Surfing Reserve — only one in Europe! Charming fishing village with great food and surf culture.

🏡 Azenhas do Mar

Stunning cliffside village with a natural ocean pool carved into the rocks. One of Portugal's most photographed spots.

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Prim

A local favourite in Ericeira's old town. Modern Portuguese with seasonal ingredients. The octopus rice and fresh fish are outstanding. Mains ~C$14–20.

📍 Rua Capitão João Lopes, Ericeira

Tik Tak

Hole-in-the-wall tasca beloved by surfers and locals. Massive portions of grilled fish at tiny prices. Order the prato do dia (daily special) — usually ~C$8–10 with soup, bread, and drink included.

📍 Ericeira old town

Mar à Vista

Seafood restaurant with actual sea views. Known for arroz de marisco and grilled dourada (gilt-head bream). Family-run, no pretension. Mains ~C$12–18.

📍 Rua Dr. Eduardo Burnay, Ericeira

Pastelaria Garrett

Local pastry shop for a morning pastel de nata and galão (milky coffee) before you ride. The bola de Berlim (custard-filled doughnut) is legendary here. ~C$2–3.

📍 Praça da República, Ericeira

Restaurante Peixe à Moda de Ericeira

The house specialty — ericeira-style fish baked with potatoes, onions, peppers & olive oil. A regional dish you won't find elsewhere. Mains ~C$12–16.

📍 Ericeira

🔧 Bike Shops

BIKESPOT & The Bike Shop

Mafra/Ericeira area — last reliable shops before Lisbon

Day 8 — Ericeira → Lisbon via Sintra & Cabo da Roca 🏁

📏 55 km📅 April 10, 2026📍 Sections 16 + 15🎉 Final day!
🔗 Sec 16 · Sec 15
🏨 Accommodation

Lisbon — Multiple Options

Hostels from €20–35/night (dorm) or €40–60 (private). Alfama, Bairro Alto, and Santos are great neighborhoods. Book in advance — Lisbon is popular!

🎯 Highlights — Sintra & Cabo da Roca

🏰 Sintra

Pena Palace, Quinta da Regaleira (initiation well!), Moorish Castle — fairy-tale UNESCO town in the hills

🌍 Cabo da Roca

Westernmost point of continental Europe! Get a certificate to prove you were here.

🏰 Mafra National Palace

Massive baroque palace & monastery — one of the largest buildings in Europe. 36,000-book library!

🎭 José Franco Village-Museum

Miniature traditional Portuguese village — charming and quirky

🦕 Praia Grande

Beach with real dinosaur footprints in the cliffs — 70+ million years old!

🏖️ Cascais

Elegant seaside town — great for a coffee stop on the final stretch

🎯 Lisbon — You Made It! 🎉

🏰 São Jorge Castle & Alfama

Hilltop castle with panoramic views. Alfama's labyrinthine streets are magical at sunset.

🚋 Tram 28

Iconic yellow tram through Lisbon's historic neighborhoods

⛪ Belém Tower & Jerónimos

Manueline masterpieces — UNESCO World Heritage. Don't miss Pastéis de Belém (custard tarts)!

🍽️ LX Factory & Time Out Market

LX Factory for creative vibes, Time Out Market for the best of Lisbon's food scene under one roof

🚆 Transport Notes

🚂 Train: Caxias → Carcavelos

Cascais commuter line — bikes allowed outside rush hour. Useful to skip a busy section into Lisbon.

⛴️ Transtejo Ferry

If approaching from south bank, take the ferry across the Tagus to Lisbon. Scenic and practical!

🍽️ Where to Eat (Local Picks)

Pastéis de Belém

THE original pastel de nata since 1837. Yes it's famous, but it's famous for a reason — the recipe is a closely guarded secret. Get them warm with cinnamon & powdered sugar. ~C$2 each. Eat inside to skip the line.

📍 Rua de Belém 84–92, Belém

Casa do Alentejo

Hidden gem in a stunning Moorish palace. Alentejano cuisine at incredibly fair prices. Try the carne de porco à alentejana (pork with clams). Mains ~C$12–16. Most tourists walk right past it.

📍 Rua das Portas de Santo Antão 58, Restauradores

Taberna da Rua das Flores

Tiny, no-reservations petiscos bar. Everything is incredible — the cured meats, the croquettes, the seasonal specials. Arrive early or wait. Petiscos ~C$5–12.

📍 Rua das Flores 103, Chiado

O Velho Eurico — Alfama

Quintessential Alfama tasca. Paper tablecloths, house wine from the barrel, grilled sardines. Sometimes fado plays. This is the real Lisbon. Mains ~C$10–14.

📍 Largo São Cristóvão 3, Alfama

Cervejaria Ramiro

THE seafood institution. Locals and chefs agree — best shellfish in Lisbon. Tiger prawns, percebes, steak sandwich to finish. Budget ~C$40–55/person but worth every cent.

📍 Av. Almirante Reis 1H, Intendente

Casa Portuguesa do Pastel de Bacalhau — Cascais

If passing through Cascais — try the pastel de bacalhau com queijo Serra da Estrela (cod cake with melted mountain cheese inside). Mind-blowing. ~C$5.

📍 Rua Frederico Arouca 30, Cascais

🍷 Lisbon food rules: Avoid Rua Augusta and the main tourist strips. The best food is always one street back. Look for "menu do dia" (daily menu) signs — full meal with drink for ~C$10–12. Alfama after 8pm = magic.
🎉 Celebration tip: You just cycled ~380 km along the Atlantic coast! Treat yourself to a sunset dinner in Alfama and a pastel de nata (or five). You've earned it.
