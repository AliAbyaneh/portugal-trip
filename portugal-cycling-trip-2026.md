# 🇵🇹 Portugal Coastal Cycling Trip: Porto → Lisbon

**Dates:** April 3–11, 2026 (9 days: 8 riding days + 1 rest day)
**Start:** Porto | **Finish:** Lisbon
**Total distance:** ~380 km riding
**Total elevation:** ~2,500m
**Bike:** Gravel bike (35-40mm tires recommended)
**Navigation:** Garmin Epix + Strava subscription for route building
**Accommodation budget:** €30–60/night

---

## ⚡ Daily Strategy

- **Sunrise:** ~7:15 AM in early April
- **Wake:** 6:30 → breakfast at hotel or nearby pastelaria
- **Ride by:** 8:00 AM latest
- **Target finish:** 13:00–14:00 (before afternoon wind/rain)
- **Why:** Atlantic coastal weather typically brings clouds/showers in the afternoon. Morning riding = better conditions, drier roads, less wind
- **Afternoon:** Explore town, eat, rest, plan next day's route

---

## 🗺 Route Overview

This route follows Portugal's Atlantic coast southbound, loosely tracking **EuroVelo 1**. The prevailing north/northwest winds push you south — Porto→Lisbon is the correct direction.

### Day 1 — April 3 (Friday): Porto → Ovar
**60 km | ~100m elevation | Flat**

#### Route Notes
- ⚠️ **Getting out of Porto is the hardest part of this entire trip.** Heavy traffic, poor cycling infrastructure in the city. Multiple cyclists say "AVOID THE CITIES."
- **Recommended:** Take the morning CP train from Porto São Bento or Campanhã to **Espinho** (~30 min, free for bikes on regional trains). Start riding from Espinho south.
- If riding out: Follow the **Ciclovia do Litoral** bike path from Foz do Douro south through Matosinhos → Vila Nova de Gaia → Espinho. Paved, flat, ocean views.
- From Espinho: quiet roads south through Esmoriz → Cortegaça → Ovar
- **Scenic lagoon detour:** From Ovar, ride east toward the **Ria de Aveiro** salt pans. The Ecovia paths along the lagoon are partially gravel — perfect for your bike. Flamingos in the lagoons near Torreira!
- Surface: mix of dedicated bike paths, quiet municipal roads, some gravel lagoon tracks

#### Highlights
- **Ciclovia do Litoral** — one of Europe's best urban coastal bike paths
- **Praia de Miramar** — Capela do Senhor da Pedra (chapel on the rocks)
- **Ria de Aveiro salt pans** — flamingos, traditional salt harvesting, beautiful light
- **Torreira** — quiet lagoon village, traditional fishing culture

#### 🏨 Accommodation in Ovar (€30–55/night)
| Option | Type | ~Price | Notes |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| **Hotel Meia Lua** | Hotel | €40–55 | Clean budget hotel in Ovar center. Good reviews. |
| **Residencial São Cristóvão** | Guesthouse | €30–40 | Simple, central, family-run. |
| **Furadouro Beach Guest House** | Guesthouse | €35–50 | In Furadouro beach area (~5km west of Ovar). Near the coast. |
| **Casa da Ria** | B&B | €40–55 | Near the lagoon area. Charming. |

*Tip: Ovar is a small town — book ahead as options are limited. Furadouro (the beach neighborhood) has more tourist accommodation.*

#### 🍽 Food in Ovar
- **Pão-de-ló de Ovar** — the town's famous sponge cake, moist and eggy. Buy at any pastelaria.
- **Restaurante O Marinheiro** — seafood, near the center
- Any local **tasca** for a *prato do dia* (daily menu: soup + main + drink, €8–10)
- Coffee at any pastelaria: €0.70 espresso + pastel de nata

### Day 2 — April 4 (Saturday): Ovar → Aveiro → Costa Nova
**40 km | ~50m elevation | Flat**

#### Route Notes
- Easy, short day — recovery from travel + Day 1
- Ride south from Ovar along the eastern shore of **Ria de Aveiro** lagoon
- Through quiet roads and lagoon-side paths to Aveiro
- Explore Aveiro ("Venice of Portugal") — canals, moliceiro boats, Art Nouveau
- Continue 10 km to **Costa Nova** — the famous candy-striped beach houses

#### Highlights
- **Ria de Aveiro** — stunning lagoon system, salt pans, moliceiro boats
- **Aveiro** — canal town, Art Nouveau architecture, university town
- **Costa Nova** — iconic striped *palheiros* (wooden houses), beautiful beach
- **São Jacinto Dunes Natural Reserve** — accessible by ferry from Aveiro (optional side trip)

### Day 4 — April 6 (Monday): Figueira da Foz → Nazaré
**60 km | ~450m elevation | Rolling coastal terrain with real climbs**

#### Route Notes

- Cross the Mondego River bridge leaving Figueira

- Stop at **São Pedro de Moel** (~35 km) — hilltop village with ocean views, great lunch spot
- Optional detour to **São Martinho do Porto** — stunning shell-shaped bay
- Final approach to Nazaré has a notable climb to the **Sítio** promontory (optional — town is at beach level)
- Avoid the N242 — use secondary roads and forest tracks

#### Highlights
- **Pinhal de Leiria** — ancient royal pine forest, atmospheric riding, good gravel
- **São Pedro de Moel** — clifftop village, lighthouse
- **São Martinho do Porto** — almost-enclosed shell-shaped bay

### Day 5 — April 7 (Tuesday): Nazaré → Peniche (via Óbidos)
**60 km | ~500m elevation | Hilliest day — rolling terrain throughout**

#### Route Notes
- ⚠️ **This section has the weakest cycling infrastructure of the whole trip.** Some road sections have more traffic and no bike lanes.
- Head south from Nazaré to **São Martinho do Porto** (if not visited yesterday, ~12 km)
- Continue through **Foz do Arelho** (where Óbidos Lagoon meets the sea)
- **Detour inland ~5 km to Óbidos** — medieval walled town, absolutely worth it
- Continue west to Peniche through rolling hills
- Mix of secondary roads and some coastal paths
- **Bail-out option:** If weather turns bad, take the train from **Caldas da Rainha** (on the route near Óbidos) to Peniche area
- Rolling hills throughout — nothing brutal but cumulative climbing adds up

#### Highlights
- **São Martinho do Porto** — shell-shaped bay, one of the calmest beaches in Portugal
- **Óbidos** — medieval walled town, white-washed houses, **ginjinha** (cherry liqueur) sold in chocolate cups. Don't skip this!
- **Lagoa de Óbidos** — Portugal's largest coastal lagoon, birdwatching
- **Peniche** — fishing town, surf capital, gateway to Berlengas Islands

### Day 8 — April 10 (Friday): Ericeira → Lisbon (via Sintra Coast)
**55 km | ~600m elevation | The grand finale — hilly then flat**

#### Route Notes
- ⭐ **The best scenery day of the trip.** Sintra coast is stunning but demanding.
- Ericeira → **Praia das Maçãs** along coastal roads/paths
- Optional detour to **Cabo da Roca** — westernmost point of mainland Europe (~5 km detour, worth it!)
- Descend through the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park toward **Cascais**
- Some good gravel options in Sintra-Cascais park — one cyclist called it "amazing as a half-day gravel ride"
- From Cascais: take the **Ciclovia do Tejo** (river bike path) east to Lisbon — flat, paved, scenic. Through Estoril, Oeiras, Belém.
- ⚠️ **Water refill spots are rare after Praia Grande on the Sintra coast.** Carry extra (2L minimum).
- ⚠️ **"The climb back toward Sintra is short but steep"** — expect 200-300m climbing

#### Alternative: Train finish
- ⚠️ **Entering Lisbon by bike is terrible** — "massive roads, some don't even have sidewalks"
- **Option:** Ride to Cascais, then take the CP train to Lisbon Cais do Sodré (~40 min, bikes allowed on regional trains)
- Or ride the riverside bike path from Cascais — it's good, just longer

#### Highlights
- **Cabo da Roca** — westernmost point of continental Europe. Get the certificate (€11, tourist trap but fun).
- **Sintra-Cascais Natural Park** — wild coastal cliffs, forest, dramatic scenery
- **Cascais** — upscale coastal town, marina, beaches
- **Belém** — Jerónimos Monastery, Tower of Belém, **Pastéis de Belém** (the original pastel de nata bakery — NON-NEGOTIABLE STOP)
- **Lisbon** — your destination! Celebrate on the Tejo waterfront 🎉

## 🗺 GPX Sources & Navigation

### Primary: Garmin Epix + Strava
- Build routes in **Strava Route Builder** (use Global Heatmap to see where cyclists actually ride)
- Sync directly to Garmin Epix via Strava-Garmin Connect integration
- Use **Strava Global Heatmap** to validate route choices — avoids dead ends and bad roads

### GPX Downloads
1. **EuroVelo 1 (Atlantic Coast):** [en.eurovelo.com/ev1/portugal](https://en.eurovelo.com/ev1/portugal) — official GPX tracks
2. **Komoot:** Search "Porto Lisbon coastal cycling" — multiple curated routes with surface type info
3. **RideWithGPS:** Search "EV1 Portugal" for community-uploaded gravel variants
4. **Strava:** Search segments and routes in each region

### Navigation Tips
- ⚠️ EuroVelo 1 is **not consistently signed** between Porto and Lisbon. Don't rely on road signs — use GPS.
- ⚠️ Some Komoot/GPS routes suggest paths that are impassable by bike — dead ends, cliff crossings that don't exist
- Download **offline maps** on Garmin + phone backup (Google Maps or OsmAnd)
- Pre-load each day's route the evening before
- **OsmAnd** (free, OpenStreetMap-based) is excellent as phone backup — shows trails and bike paths

## ⚠️ Key Warnings & Safety Notes

### 🚨 Critical
1. **Porto exit is dangerous.** Take the train to Espinho/Ovar if possible. Heavy traffic, poor cycling infrastructure.
2. **Lisbon entry is dangerous.** Consider train from Cascais to Cais do Sodré. Or use the riverside bike path from Cascais (which is actually good).
3. **Nazaré → Peniche has the weakest infrastructure.** Some busy roads with no shoulder. Be alert, use lights.
4. **Water is scarce on the Sintra coast** after Praia Grande. Fill up before the coastal section (Day 8).
5. **GPS is essential.** Route signage is unreliable. Always have a charged Garmin + phone backup.

### ⚠️ Important
6. **Buses don't reliably take bikes.** Flixbus may refuse unboxed bikes despite policy. Use trains instead.
7. **Trains:** CP regional trains take bikes for free (space permitting). **Alfa Pendular (fast trains) do NOT take bikes.** Use Intercidades or Regional services.
8. **Dead-end paths exist.** Some "EuroVelo" paths terminate at cliffs or water crossings that don't exist. Always have GPS.
9. **Afternoon wind/weather.** Atlantic coast gets windier and potentially wetter in the afternoon. Ride mornings.
10. **Traffic on N-roads.** Avoid national roads (N1, N242, etc.) where possible. Secondary CM roads are much better.

### Train Info (CP - Comboios de Portugal)
- **Porto Campanhã → Espinho:** Regional, ~30 min, bikes free
- **Porto Campanhã → Ovar:** Regional, ~45 min, bikes free  
- **Caldas da Rainha → Peniche area:** Bus connection (bikes difficult)
- **Cascais → Lisbon Cais do Sodré:** Cascais line, ~40 min, bikes allowed off-peak
- **Book/check:** [cp.pt](https://www.cp.pt)
- Tip: Avoid rush hours (8-9 AM, 5-7 PM) when bikes may be refused due to space

## 🛠 Practical Tips

### SIM Cards & Connectivity
- Buy a **Vodafone**, **MEO**, or **NOS** prepaid SIM at Porto airport or any phone shop
- ~€15–20 for 10–15 GB data + calls for 30 days
- Vodafone has the best coastal coverage
- Free WiFi at most accommodations and many cafés
- **MB Way** app — Portugal's ubiquitous mobile payment system (works with Portuguese SIM)

### Bike Shops Along the Route
| Location | Shop | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Porto** | Órbita Bikes, Bikeshed Porto, Decathlon (Gaia) | Full service |
| **Aveiro** | Decathlon Aveiro, local shops | Basic to full service |
| **Figueira da Foz** | Limited — sport shops only | Basic repairs |
| **Nazaré** | Very limited | Carry spares |
| **Peniche** | Very limited | Carry spares |
| **Ericeira** | Limited but growing | Basic repairs |
| **Lisbon** | Lisbon Bike Hub, Órbita, Decathlon (multiple) | Full service |

**⚠️ Between Aveiro and Lisbon, bike-specific shops are sparse. Carry essential spares.**

### Emergency Contacts
- **Emergency (police/fire/ambulance):** 112
- **Tourist police:** +351 21 342 1634
- **Canadian Embassy Lisbon:** +351 21 316 4600
- **Roadside/general help:** 800 202 232
- **CP trains info:** +351 707 210 220

### General Tips
- **Prevailing wind is your friend.** NW winds push you south — correct direction ✓
- **Siesta:** Small shops/restaurants close 13:00–15:00. Plan lunch accordingly.
- **Coffee:** Espresso (*café* or *bica* in Lisbon) = €0.60–0.80 everywhere. Your fuel.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Round up or leave €1–2 at restaurants.
- **Portuguese basics:** *Obrigado* (thanks, male), *Com licença* (excuse me), *Água, por favor* (water please), *Onde fica...?* (where is...?)
- **Tap water:** Safe and drinkable throughout Portugal. Refill at cafés, restaurants, public fountains (*água potável*).
- **Hotel bike storage:** Generally fine — confirm when booking. Most places will let you bring the bike inside.
- **Luggage transfer:** If you want to ride light, [LuggageTransfers.com](https://luggagetransfers.com) or local operators shuttle bags for ~€15–25/stage.

### Getting Your Bike to Portugal
- Most airlines: bikes as checked luggage €30–75 each way in a bike box/bag
- Get a free bike box from a local bike shop, or use a reusable travel case
- **Alternative:** Rent a gravel bike in Porto, drop off in Lisbon — check with local rental shops (graveltravel.pt offers rentals)

## 🔑 Summary: The 5 Things That Matter Most

1. **Train out of Porto** (skip the urban nightmare) and **train into Lisbon from Cascais** (skip the dangerous approach)
2. **Ride mornings** — out by 8:00, done by 14:00. Afternoon weather deteriorates.
3. **Carry water on the Sintra coast** (Day 8) and **carry bike spares** (no shops Nazaré→Ericeira)
4. **GPS is non-negotiable** — signs are unreliable, dead-end paths exist
5. **Don't skip Óbidos, Cabo da Roca, and Pastéis de Belém** — they're the trip highlights along with the riding itself
